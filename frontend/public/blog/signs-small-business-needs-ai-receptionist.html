7 Signs Your Small Business Needs an AI Receptionist (And How to Choose One)

Every missed call is a missed opportunity. Here's how to know when it's time to automate your phone answering—and how to pick the right solution.

The Real Cost of Missed Calls

Small businesses lose an estimated $60 billion annually due to missed calls. When a potential customer calls and gets voicemail—or worse, no answer at all—they don't wait. They call your competitor.

Here are the hard numbers:

  • 67% of callers hang up when they reach voicemail
  • 80% of customers expect an immediate response
  • 60 seconds is the average time before a caller gives up
  • $1,200 average lifetime value of a single missed business inquiry

7 Warning Signs You Need an AI Receptionist

1. You're Missing Calls During Business Hours

If you're constantly in meetings, on job sites, or serving other customers, your phone rings unanswered. An AI receptionist answers every call—every time.

2. After-Hours Calls Go to Voicemail

Your customers don't stop calling at 5 PM. If you're only answering 40% of your calls (business hours only), you're losing 60% of potential business.

3. You're Spending $2,000+/Month on a Human Receptionist

A full-time receptionist costs $30,000-$40,000/year plus benefits. AI receptionists start at $299/month and work 24/7.

4. You're Using Your Personal Cell for Business

Mixing personal and business calls kills professionalism. An AI receptionist gives you a dedicated business number with branded messaging.

5. You're the Bottleneck for Appointments

Every booking requires you to answer the phone. An AI agent books directly into your calendar automatically.

6. You're Too Busy to Follow Up on Missed Calls

You see the missed call notification but can't call back for hours. By then, the prospect has moved on.

7. You're Losing Customers to Competitors

If you've ever heard "I called but no one answered, so I went with [competitor]," you need an AI receptionist yesterday.
